1914
The start of World War One

World War One initiated due to the assasination of the heir to the Austro-Hungarian Empire, Archduke Franz Ferdinand. Because of this, Serbia was declared war on by Austria-Hungary, beginning the first world war. Luxembourg and Belgium were invaded by Germany while Alsace was invaded by France. Forces from Great Britain arrive in France. There were many nations allied against Germany, and the allied forces began to increase.

1915
German Naval and Zeppelin attacks

There is a naval blockade of Great Britain (Britain forces are surrounded) begun by Germany. German Zeppelin attacks were blunted by Birmingham. Landing of the Allied forces on the Gallipoli Peninsula ofo the Ottoman Empire takes place. During a crossing of the passenger liner Lusitania from New York to Liverpool, England, a German submarine sinks it, killing 128 Americans and causing great furiosity for the United States. Italy declared war on Austria-Hungary.

1916
Battle of Verdun

Attack on Verdun is begun by Germany on February 21, 1916. It continues until December 18, 1916, ending with 550000 French casualties and 450000 German casualties. While that was going on, the Naval Battle of Jutland occured between British and German fleets.

1917
United States declares war

After the halt of unrestricted submarine warfare after sinking of Lusitania, Germany finally returns to it on February 1, 1917. Two days later, the United States severs diplomatic relations with Germany. And on April 6, 1917, the United States declares war on Germany. General John J. Pershing becomes the newly selected commander of the American Expeditionary Forces about two months later; he arrives in England with his staff. Soon after, American combat forces arrive in France. It is not until mid-December when Russia signs armstice with Germany.
